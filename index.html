Voting at a polling station

1

Check you're registered

You must be on the electoral roll for Canbury Ward. If you're unsure, contact Kingston Council's Electoral Registration Office or check at GOV.UK.

2

Find your polling station

Your polling card will state your assigned polling station. If you've lost it, look up your station at GOV.UK using your address. Stations are open 7am – 10pm on election day.

3

Bring valid photo ID

Since the Elections Act 2022, you must bring an accepted form of photo ID. Accepted forms include: passport, driving licence, Blue Badge, older person's bus pass, and others. Full list at GOV.UK.

4

Mark your ballot paper

In a First Past the Post election, you select one candidate only by marking a cross (✗) in the box next to their name. Voting for more than one candidate will spoil your ballot.

5

Place your ballot in the box

Fold your ballot paper and place it in the ballot box. The count takes place after polls close at 10pm on 7 May 2026.

About First Past the Post

In this election you vote for one candidate only. The candidate with the most votes wins the seat — there is no requirement to achieve a majority. This is the standard method for UK local council elections.

There are no ranked choices, preferences, or elimination rounds. Your single vote is your full and complete contribution to the result.

Results: The count will take place after polls close on 7 May 2026. Results will be announced by the Kingston upon Thames Returning Officer and published on the council website.
